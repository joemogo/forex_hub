Auto Paper Trading
When ON, every scan cycle (~60s) checks every Active Watch pair against your full rule set, and opens a paper trade the moment everything lines up — no clicking needed. Conditions checked, all required:
  • Pair is on your Active Watch list (set via Sunday Scan — top-down bias already agreed)
  • Session is active (London or New York — no off-hours entries)
  • Live confluence score ≥ 55% in the signal's direction
  • A fresh engulfing candle trigger matching that direction and bias just fired
  • A valid AOI (support for buys / resistance for sells) exists to set the stop
  • Resulting trade is at least 1:2 R:R
  • No trade already open on that pair, and it hasn't already auto-traded that pair today
This always checks its own dedicated 15M candles for the entry trigger, no matter what timeframe you have selected on the Scanner chart — feel free to browse any timeframe while it's running.

Backtest your rule set
Replays your exact rules bar-by-bar through real historical 15M candles: confluence ≥ 55%, a fresh engulfing candle matching bias, a valid AOI, session active, and R:R ≥ 2. Every time all of those align, it's counted as a trigger, then price is walked forward to see whether the target or stop got hit first.
Two approximations worth knowing: (1) live trading sources AOI from weekly/daily structure and bias from your 3-timeframe Sunday Scan — reconstructing both at every single historical bar isn't practical here, so backtesting computes a rolling bias and AOI zone from the same 15M data being replayed instead (same mechanical trend engine as Automatic Top-Down Scan, just applied to 15M rather than true weekly/daily). Results are directionally honest and historically accurate — the bias genuinely varies bar to bar as it would have at the time — but won't be pixel-identical to live Auto Trading's exact zone placement.

Optimizer — find the best confluence combination
Tests 108 combinations of confluence threshold, minimum R:R, engulf strictness (1 or 2 consecutive candles swallowed — 1 is the standard textbook definition), day-of-week filter (all week vs. your stated Mon-Wed preference), and stop-loss buffer size (3/7/12 pips beyond the AOI edge — previously fixed at 7 with no testing at all) — each pooled across all 12 pairs at the lookback selected above. Ranked by expectancy in R (average R gained or lost per trade), not win rate alone, since a lower-win-rate setup with bigger average wins can still be the better one.
Read the trade count on every row before trusting a result. A combination showing a great expectancy off 3 trades is not evidence of anything — it's noise. Look for combinations that hold up with a real trade count, not just the top of the list.

Explore new confluence factors
Tests two factors you don't currently have at all, layered on top of the best-known baseline (55% confluence, 1.5:1 R:R, standard 1-candle engulf, all week, 7-pip stop): multi-timeframe AOI overlap — straight from your own rule that a weekly AOI and daily AOI overlapping is the highest-probability entry, which wasn't implemented anywhere until now — and an ATR-based volatility filter, a standard technique that avoids both dead-quiet chop and volatility spikes, never tried in this app before.
Uses the lookback selected above — longer history (180/365 days) gives these new, likely-stricter filters a real chance to show enough trades to judge.
